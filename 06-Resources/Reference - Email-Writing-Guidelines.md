# Email Writing Guidelines

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-27
**Purpose:** Rules for drafting emails to team, partners, and business contacts. Structure, tone, formatting, and validation.

---

## Structure

| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| **Subject line** | State the decision, action, or outcome. Avoid vague ("Quick question") or generic ("Update"). |
| **Lead sentence** | Put the main point in the first line. |
| **Paragraphs** | Short (1–4 sentences). One idea per paragraph. |
| **Length** | Keep under ~150 words when possible. Longer emails need a TL;DR at the top. |
| **Lists** | Use bullets for options, action items, or multi-part asks. |
| **Closing** | End with the concrete ask or outcome. Avoid filler sign-offs with no purpose. |

---

## Tone by Recipient

| Audience | Tone | Avoid |
|----------|------|-------|
| **Team** | Direct, lean, calm. Assume shared context -- cut anything they already know. | Over-explaining, lecturing. |
| **Partners** | Clear, collaborative. State expectations and commitments. They care about outcomes, not theory. | Vague commitments, ambiguity on ownership. |
| **Business contacts** | Professional, concise. They skim -- put the payoff early. | Hype, long intros, buried asks. |

---

## Formatting

*Universal voice rules from CLAUDE.md Drafting Style apply (no contractions, no em-dashes, short sentences). The rules below are email-specific.*

- Short paragraphs (1–4 sentences). One blank line between paragraphs.
- No emojis in formal or external emails.
- No jargon unless the audience uses it. Spell acronyms on first use.

---

## Content Rules

| Do | Avoid |
|----|-------|
| Lead with outcome, decision, or ask | Long preamble before the point |
| State action items and owners explicitly | Vague "let us discuss" with no next step |
| Separate FYI from action requests | Blending "FYI" with "please do X" in one muddled ask |
| One main topic per email when possible | Multiple unrelated requests in one message |
| Pick a side when a decision is needed | Fence-sitting when a choice is required |

---

## Action Items and Ownership

- Format: "Action: [what], Owner: [who], Due: [when]."
- One clear ask per email when possible.
- If you need a response, say by when and what you need (approval, input, decision).

---

## Subject Line Patterns

- **Decision:** "Decision: [topic]: [outcome]"
- **Action needed:** "Action needed: [what] by [when]"
- **Info share:** "[Topic]: [one-line summary]"
- **Meeting follow-up:** "[Date] [Topic]: decisions and actions"

---

## Thread Management

- Change the subject line if the topic has shifted from the original thread.
- Do not bury a new ask inside a long reply thread. Start a new email.

---

## Pre-Send Validation

1. **Point:** Can the reader state the main point from the first few lines alone?
2. **Action:** If you need something from them, is the ask explicit?
3. **Tone:** Does it match the relationship (team vs partner vs contact)?
4. **Length:** Is anything removable without losing meaning?
5. **Subject:** Does the subject line reflect the content?

---

## Templates by Type

### Decision or Update to Team

```
Subject: [Decision/Topic]: [one-line outcome]

[Main point in first sentence.]

[Brief context if needed.]

[Action items if any.]
```

### Request to Partner or Contact

```
Subject: [Topic]: [specific ask]

[What you are asking for, in one sentence.]

[One or two lines of context.]

[What you need from them, by when.]
```

### Meeting Follow-Up

```
Subject: [Meeting name]: decisions and actions

Decisions: [list]
Actions: [who / what / when]
Open items: [if any]
Next: [next meeting or step]
```
