# Areas

Ongoing domains of life and work that require maintenance but never "finish." Areas give context and continuity — projects live inside them.

## Structure

```
05-Areas/
├── Activities-Social/          # Hobbies, leisure, social life → Documents\Activities
├── Body-Mind-Spirit/           # Wellbeing and personal development → Documents\Body Mind Spirit
├── Life-Ops/                   # Life administration → Documents\Life Ops
│   ├── Finances/
│   └── Admin/
├── PPM-Career/                 # Startup operator career → Documents\PPM Career
│   ├── Brand-and-Positioning/  # North Star: identity, positioning, priorities
│   ├── Professional-Development/
│   │   └── Job-Search/
│   ├── Thought-Leadership/     # Artifacts: posts, speaking decks, book drafts
│   ├── Marketing-and-Sales/    # Collateral, campaigns for Emerging Humanity
│   └── Clients-and-Startups/
├── Side-Ventures/              # Other ventures (Directory Website, Music publishing) → Documents\Side Ventures
├── Wealth/                     # Investing, real estate, passive income → Documents\Wealth
└── People/                     # Relationships across all areas (Internal, External)
    ├── Internal/
    └── External/
```

## Areas vs Projects

**Area** = ongoing responsibility with no end date ("Stay fit", "Maintain client relationships", "Build professional brand")

**Project** = specific initiative with a finish line ("Run 10K by March", "Cognome ExplainerAI Q1 delivery", "File 2025 taxes")

Projects belong to an area — they're how you make progress within it. When a project completes, it moves to `07-Archives/`. The area stays.

## People

`People/` is kept at the top level because relationships cross every domain — you have people in your professional life, engagements, social life, and personal world. It doesn't belong nested under any single area.

- **Internal/** — Colleagues and close collaborators (same email domain)
- **External/** — Clients, partners, service providers, contacts from other domains

## Adding New Areas

Create a new folder under the relevant top-level area when a domain grows enough to warrant its own space. A new sailing club, a new client engagement, a new coaching relationship — add a file or subfolder where it belongs.
