# Phase 4 — Late Interview Strategy: Differentiation & Contribution

**Goal:** Prove you are already operating as part of the team. Shift from performing to co-creating.

**Game 2 focus:** Get Filtered In. Demonstrate contribution and ownership.

**Identity layering:** Authority from outcomes, not performance. Use language at altitude: "I decide" not "I suggest"; "This stops" not "Maybe we should pause." Humanity inside authority — warm in delivery, decisive in substance.

---

## Step 1: Competition & Challenge Mapping

Research:
- Recent deals, competitive threats, transformation pain points
- Tie each to potential 30/60/90-day objectives

---

## Step 2: 30-60-90 Day Strategic Plan

Customized to the employer's diagnosed symptoms and KPIs from Phase 1.

Structure:
- **Day 1–30:** Learn — key stakeholders, systems, current state
- **Day 31–60:** Contribute — first meaningful deliverable
- **Day 61–90:** Lead — early strategic initiative

---

## Step 3: "Ideas They Haven't Thought Of" One-Pager

2–3 opportunities or gaps identified through research that demonstrate:
- External perspective
- Strategic awareness
- Genuine interest in the company's success

---

## Step 4: Interview Simulation Chamber

Run a stress-test mock session using:
- Harder, more ambiguous questions
- Case-style prompts based on their business situation
- Challenge-the-candidate pressure scenarios

---

## Step 5: Leave-Behind Deck

Outline an executive summary for a leave-behind document:
- 30/60/90 Plan summary
- Opportunities identified
- Vision for the role

---

## Step 6: Closing Impact Move

Draft a closing statement that:
- Articulates understanding of their core problem
- Proposes a forward-looking next step
- Invites collaboration: "Here's what I'd want to explore in my first month..."
